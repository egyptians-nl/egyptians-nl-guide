# FAQ

Below are common early questions from Egyptians living in the Netherlands.

## Do I need a BSN before I can do most official things?

In many cases, yes. A BSN is commonly needed for work, taxes, health insurance, banking, and access to many government systems.

## Should I get DigiD as soon as possible?

Usually yes. DigiD becomes useful quickly for tax matters, municipality services, health insurance matters, and other public services.

## Can I live somewhere if I cannot register at the address?

Be careful. Not being able to register can affect important parts of your legal and practical setup. Understand the consequences before agreeing.

## Is Dutch health insurance always required?

Many residents need it, but the exact rule depends on the situation. Do not guess. Check the official rules for your case.

## What number do I call in an emergency?

Call `112` for urgent emergencies.

## Can I rely on advice from friends or social media about permits?

No. Personal experiences can be helpful, but permit rules must be verified through IND because outcomes depend on the exact route and conditions.

## Why is my net salary lower than the gross salary?

Because tax and other deductions may be taken from gross pay before you receive the final amount.

## Can I work freelance on any residence permit?

Not necessarily. Some residence situations may limit or condition self-employment. Verify the rules attached to your own permit.

## Community guide disclaimer

This is a community guide, not legal, immigration, tax, or medical advice. For immigration, check IND. For taxes, check Belastingdienst. For healthcare, check official Dutch healthcare sources or your insurer.
