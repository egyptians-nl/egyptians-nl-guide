# Healthcare

Dutch healthcare is structured and generally starts with the GP, called the `huisarts`. Understanding the basics helps you get help faster when you need it.

## Start with a GP (`huisarts`)

For non-emergency care, the GP is usually your first point of contact. A GP can:

- Assess symptoms
- Give treatment or advice
- Refer you to a specialist if needed
- Keep your main medical file

Try to register with a GP near your home early, because some practices have waiting lists.

## Health insurance basics

Most residents need Dutch basic health insurance. The insurer, coverage options, and deductible can affect your costs and access.

Review:

- The monthly premium
- The yearly deductible
- Covered providers and pharmacies
- Reimbursement rules

## Emergency numbers

In an emergency, call `112`.

For urgent medical help outside GP office hours, there is often a `huisartsenpost` in your region. Check how this works in your area before you need it.

## Mental healthcare basics

Mental healthcare may begin through your GP, who can discuss symptoms and refer you if needed. Coverage and waiting times vary, so check your insurance details carefully.

If you need immediate help in a crisis, use emergency channels or urgent local care options.

## Community guide disclaimer

This is a community guide, not medical advice. For healthcare decisions, check official Dutch healthcare sources, your GP, or your insurer.
