# Getting Started

The first weeks in the Netherlands usually involve paperwork, digital accounts, and basic services. Taking the steps in the right order can save time.

## 1. Register at the municipality

In most cases, one of the first steps is registering your address with the local municipality (`gemeente`). This registration is often required to be entered into the Dutch personal records database.

Bring the documents the municipality asks for, which may include:

- Passport or ID
- Residence documents, if applicable
- Birth or marriage certificates in some cases
- Your rental contract or proof of address

Rules differ by municipality, so always check your city website before your appointment.

## 2. Get your BSN

After registration, you will usually receive a BSN (`Burgerservicenummer`). This is an important personal number used for work, taxes, banking, healthcare, and many government services.

Keep it private and store it safely. You will need it often.

## 3. Create a DigiD

DigiD is the digital login used for many Dutch government and public-service websites. Once you have a BSN and meet the requirements, apply for a DigiD as early as possible.

You may need DigiD for:

- Tax matters
- Health insurance matters
- Municipality services
- IND-related online services

## 4. Arrange health insurance

Many residents are required to take out Dutch basic health insurance. Do not assume travel insurance or foreign insurance is enough.

Compare:

- Monthly premium
- Deductible (`eigen risico`)
- Whether your preferred providers are covered

## 5. Open a bank account

A Dutch or EU-compatible bank account makes daily life easier for salary payments, rent, bills, and insurance. Some banks may ask for:

- Passport or ID
- BSN
- Proof of address
- Residence document, depending on your situation

## Community guide disclaimer

This is a community guide, not legal, immigration, tax, or medical advice. For immigration matters, check IND. For taxes, check Belastingdienst. For healthcare, check official Dutch healthcare sources or your insurer.
