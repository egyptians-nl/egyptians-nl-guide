# Work

Working in the Netherlands involves more than finding a job. You will usually need the right registration, documents, and basic understanding of contracts and taxes.

## BSN for work

A BSN is often needed for payroll, tax records, and many employer processes. If you are starting employment, make sure your registration and BSN status are in order.

## Read your contract

Before accepting a job, review:

- Job title and duties
- Salary and payment frequency
- Working hours
- Trial period
- Contract length
- Notice period
- Holiday allowance and other benefits

If something is unclear, ask for clarification in writing.

## Salary and taxes

Your gross salary is not the same as your net salary. Deductions may include tax and social contributions. Check your payslips and keep copies.

If your situation is unusual, or you have income from multiple sources, verify tax matters with Belastingdienst or a qualified advisor.

## Freelancing basics

If you plan to work as a freelancer or self-employed person, there may be extra steps such as registration, invoicing, bookkeeping, and tax obligations.

Do not assume freelance work is allowed under every residence situation. Check the conditions tied to your permit.

## Community guide disclaimer

This is a community guide, not legal, immigration, or tax advice. For tax matters, check Belastingdienst. For permit-related work rights, verify with IND.
